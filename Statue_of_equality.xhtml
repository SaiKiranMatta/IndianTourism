Statue Of Equality

The Statue of Equality is a statue of the 11th-century Indian philosopher Ramanuja, located on the premises of the Chinna Jeeyar Trust at Muchintal Ranga Reddy district in the outskirts of Hyderabad It is the second tallest sitting statue in the world. The project of building the statue was conceptualised by the trust to commemorate 1,000 year birth anniversary of Ramanuja, The base building hosts a vedic digital library, research centre, ancient Indian texts, theatre and a gallery. Ramanuja's works are presented in the gallery.

There are 108 steps leading to the statue which provide stunning panoramic views. There are also 36 beautifully carved elephants near the statue, each spouting cool water along with 108 Divya Desa centres. The ground floor of the base statue building is dedicated to the life and teachings of Sri Ramanuja. The second floor is a temple, and the third floor contains a Vedic library and research centre. The Statue of Equality is located near the Hyderabad Airport
