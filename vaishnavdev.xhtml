Vaishno Devi - Goddess Of Hills

Gulmarg is the crowning glory of Jammu & Kashmir Vaishno Devi also known as Mata Rani is a manifestation of the Hindu mother goddess Durga The words "Maa" and "Mata" are commonly used in India for mother, and thus are often heavily used in connection with the goddess. It is believed that during the pooja and aarti, Goddesses arrive at the Holy cave to pay their respect to Mata Rani. Devotees believe the goddess herself calls the devotees to reach here

The darshan of Maa Vaishno Devi in the holy cave is in the form of three naturally formed rocks These pindies manifest the three forms of the goddess as Maha Kali, Maha Saraswati and Maha Lakshmi. More than one crore devotees visit the Vaishno Devi temple each year The Vaishno Devi Temple is an important Hindu temple dedicated to Vaishno Devi located in the Trikuta Mountains The temple is one of the 108 Shakti Peethas dedicated to Durga, who is worshipped as Vaishno Devi.
