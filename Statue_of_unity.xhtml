Statue of Unity

The Statue of Unity is the world's tallest statue, with a height of 182 metres, located near Kevadia in the state of Gujarat, India. It depicts Indian statesman and independence activist Vallabhbhai Patel, who was the first deputy prime minister and home minister of independent India and an adherent of Mahatma Gandhi.

The Statue of Unity, the world's tallest monument, is a remarkable creation symbolizing the unity and strength of India. Standing at an impressive height, it pays tribute to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, a key figure in India's freedom struggle and the unification of princely states. This engineering marvel was completed in just 46 months and serves as a powerful reminder of Sardar Patel's pivotal role. The statue has become a significant landmark, attracting visitors from around the globe. It stands as a testament to India's rich cultural heritage and the unwavering spirit of its people.
